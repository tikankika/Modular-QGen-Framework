```markdown
#### Bloom's Level Decision Guide

**When uncertain between levels:**

**Remember vs. Understand:**
- Remember: Can they recall/recognize without explanation? â†’ Remember
- Understand: Must they explain WHY or HOW? â†’ Understand

**Understand vs. Apply:**
- Understand: Explaining a concept in their own words â†’ Understand
- Apply: Using the concept in a NEW scenario â†’ Apply

**Apply vs. Analyze:**
- Apply: Using a procedure/method in a situation â†’ Apply
- Analyze: Breaking down relationships, comparing/contrasting â†’ Analyze

**Analyze vs. Evaluate:**
- Analyze: Examining components and relationships â†’ Analyze
- Evaluate: Making judgments with justification â†’ Evaluate

**Default to lower level when uncertain** - easier to assess validly.
```

**Purpose:** Provides decision criteria for distinguishing between Bloom's levels in ambiguous cases, with default guidance to lower levels for assessment validity.

```markdown
#### Objective Count Guidelines

**Target Ranges:**
- Total objectives: 15-30 (depending on course scope)
- Tier 1: 10-20 objectives (majority of assessment)
- Tier 2: 3-8 objectives (supplementary assessment)
- Tier 3: 0-2 objectives (background, if included)

**Quality over Quantity:**
- Better to have 15 well-crafted objectives than 40 vague ones
- Each objective should be specific, measurable, and evidence-based
- Combine related learning goals rather than splitting unnecessarily

**Red Flags:**
- <10 total objectives: Probably too few, missing important learning goals
- >40 total objectives: Too many, likely splitting hairs or including background content
```

**Purpose:** Provides target ranges for objective counts, emphasizes quality over quantity, and identifies red flags for too many/too few objectives.

### 4. New Section: "Systematic Presentation Strategy" (12 lines)
**Location:** At beginning of Validation Dialogue in PART B  
**Lines Added:** 12 lines (including separator)

**Content Added:**
```markdown
**Systematic Presentation Strategy:**

1. **Present by topic** (not by Bloom's level) - keeps context clear
2. **Show all objectives for a topic together** - teacher sees complete picture
3. **Highlight evidence and examples** for each objective
4. **After each topic, pause for feedback** before moving to next
5. **Take notes on requested changes** but don't interrupt flow
6. **After all topics presented, go back and make revisions**

This prevents fragmentation and allows teacher to see the complete learning story 
for each topic before commenting.
```

**Purpose:** Guides systematic presentation of all objectives, maintaining topic context and allowing comprehensive feedback before revisions.
