**Current State:**
- Integration Logic says "2-4 objectives per major concept" for Tier 1
- Says "1-2 objectives per concept" for Tier 2
- BUT doesn't give total target or guidance on avoiding too many/too few

**Recommendation:**
ADD "Objective Count Guidelines" section in PART B after Integration Logic (8-10 lines):
```markdown
### Objective Count Guidelines

**Target Ranges:**
- Total objectives: 15-30 (depending on course scope)
- Tier 1: 10-20 objectives (majority of assessment)
- Tier 2: 3-8 objectives (supplementary assessment)
- Tier 3: 0-2 objectives (background, if included)

**Quality over Quantity:**
- Better to have 15 well-crafted objectives than 40 vague ones
- Each objective should be specific, measurable, and evidence-based
- Combine related learning goals rather than splitting unnecessarily

**Red Flags:**
- <10 total objectives: Probably too few, missing important learning goals
- >40 total objectives: Too many, likely splitting hairs or including background content
```

**Recommendation:**

```markdown
#### Systematic Presentation Strategy

**How to Present Objectives:**
1. **Present by topic** (not by Bloom's level) - keeps context clear
2. **Show all objectives for a topic together** - teacher sees complete picture
3. **Highlight evidence and examples** for each objective
4. **After each topic, pause for feedback** before moving to next
5. **Take notes on requested changes** but don't interrupt flow
6. **After all topics presented, go back and make revisions**

This prevents fragmentation and allows teacher to see the complete learning story for 
each topic before commenting.
```

### Gap 5: No Bloom's Level Decision Guide for Edge Cases

**Current State:**
- Bloom's Taxonomy Application shows verbs for each level
- FOR TEACHERS section says "Claude will suggest the correct level"
- BUT no guidance for Claude on distinguishing between levels when ambiguous

**Recommendation:**
ADD "Bloom's Level Decision Guide" after Bloom's Taxonomy Application (12-15 lines):
```markdown
### Bloom's Level Decision Guide

**When uncertain between levels:**

**Remember vs. Understand:**
- Remember: Can they recall/recognize without explanation? ‚Üí Remember
- Understand: Must they explain WHY or HOW? ‚Üí Understand

**Understand vs. Apply:**
- Understand: Explaining a concept in their own words ‚Üí Understand
- Apply: Using the concept in a NEW scenario ‚Üí Apply

**Apply vs. Analyze:**
- Apply: Using a procedure/method in a situation ‚Üí Apply
- Analyze: Breaking down relationships, comparing/contrasting ‚Üí Analyze

**Analyze vs. Evaluate:**
- Analyze: Examining components and relationships ‚Üí Analyze
- Evaluate: Making judgments with justification ‚Üí Evaluate

**Default to lower level when uncertain** - easier to assess validly.
```

**Priority:** LOW TO MEDIUM  
**Lines to add:** ~15 lines
